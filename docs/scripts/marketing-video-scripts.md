### Video 4.2 — "Cooking Your First Roast — Sunday Dinner" (5 min)

**Setting:** Kitchen. Ben + Phoebe (or partner). Casual. Real ingredients. No music. Real-time cooking, condensed.

---

[BEN AT THE COUNTER. A CHUCK ROAST ON A CUTTING BOARD.]

**BEN (0:00):**
If you've never cooked a roast bigger than a meatloaf, this is for you. We're doing a chuck roast — about three pounds. Three-hour cook. Six people fed.

[ON-SCREEN INGREDIENT LIST appears: "3-lb chuck roast, salt, pepper, 1 onion, 4 garlic cloves, 2 carrots, 2 cups beef broth, 2 tbsp tomato paste, fresh rosemary, butter, flour"]

**BEN (0:15):**
Ingredients on screen. Most of this you have. Pause if you need to grab anything.

[BEN PATS THE ROAST DRY.]

**BEN (0:30):**
Step one — dry it. Pat the roast dry with a paper towel. Wet meat doesn't brown. Brown meat tastes better. Don't skip this.

[BEN SEASONS HEAVILY.]

**BEN (0:45):**
Step two — season. Salt heavy. Pepper heavy. Both sides. More than you think. A three-pound roast is dense — most of the seasoning doesn't make it to the inside, so the outside has to carry.

[BEN HEATS A DUTCH OVEN.]

**BEN (1:00):**
Step three — sear. Dutch oven on high heat. Two tablespoons of butter or oil. When it shimmers, roast in. Don't move it for three minutes a side. You want a dark brown crust — that's the flavor.

[REAL-TIME SEAR, ROAST SIZZLING. CUT TO 6 MIN LATER.]

**BEN (1:30):**
Both sides seared. Pull the roast out, set aside.

[ONIONS GOING IN.]

**BEN (1:45):**
Step four — vegetables. Onion in, rough chop. Garlic in, smashed not minced. Carrots in, big chunks. Cook for three minutes till the onion is translucent.

**BEN (2:00):**
Step five — tomato paste. Two tablespoons. Cook it for a minute against the bottom of the pot. This is the depth.

**BEN (2:15):**
Step six — deglaze. Two cups of beef broth. Scrape the bottom of the pot with a wooden spoon — that brown stuff is flavor. All of it goes back in.

[BEN RETURNS THE ROAST TO THE POT, ADDS ROSEMARY.]

**BEN (2:35):**
Roast back in. Rosemary on top, two sprigs. Lid on. Oven, three hundred twenty-five degrees, three hours.

[ON-SCREEN: "3 HOURS LATER"]

[CUT TO BEN PULLING THE ROAST OUT. STEAM RISING.]

**BEN (3:00):**
Three hours later. The roast is tender — fork goes in with no resistance. That's how you know it's done.

**BEN (3:15):**
Step seven — rest. Take it out of the pot, ten minutes on a board. Don't slice yet.
